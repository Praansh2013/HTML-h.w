Venus

Hottest planet

Venus is the second planet from the Sun, a terrestrial planet known for being the hottest in our solar system, and is sometimes called Earth's twin due to its similar size and mass. Here's a more detailed look at Venus: Key Facts: Location: Second planet from the Sun, after Mercury and before Earth. Size and Mass: Venus is similar in size and mass to Earth, earning it the nickname "Earth's twin". Atmosphere: It has a very thick, dense atmosphere, composed mostly of carbon dioxide with a global sulfuric acid cloud cover. Temperature: Venus is the hottest planet in our solar system, with surface temperatures reaching over 460 degrees Celsius (860 degrees Fahrenheit). Rotation: Venus rotates in the opposite direction compared to Earth and most other planets. Surface: Venus has an active surface with volcanoes and a heavily cratered terrain. Brightness: Venus is the brightest planet in the night sky, sometimes appearing as the "morning star" or "evening star". Naming: Venus is named after the Roman goddess of love and beauty, who was known as Aphrodite to the ancient Greeks. Orbit: Venus orbits the sun at a distance of about 67 million miles (108 million kilometers).
